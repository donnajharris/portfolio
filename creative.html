Piano

I can play several instruments, but the piano is definitely my first love and most proficient instrument.

Playing since I could first reach the keyboard, I am a classically-trained pianist. I have a Grade 10 Piano Certificate from the Royal Conversatory of Music (Toronto, Canada). I also successfully completed the Grade 3 and 4 Keyboard Harmony courses. I can play "by ear", from chord charts, lead sheets, and written manuscripts, and I'm comfortable with a wide range of styles.

My regular piano responsibilities continue as a long-time piano pianist and accompanist for The Salvation Army Guelph Citadel church services and church choir (known as the Songsters). I also have experience accompanying soloists for special occasions, post-secondary auditions and performance juries, and the Guelph Kiwanis Festival.

Piano Album: Winter Storm Project

Winter Storm Project.

The "Winter Storm Project" of 2018 was a self-produced Christmas present for a group of my friends. I asked for their favourite carols and played improvisations with little to no editing. The styles were quite varied and this playlist shows that diversity.

Some of these tracks were used by The Salvation Army in Canada and Bermuda on their annual Christmas compilation recording in 2019 (What Child is This? and Hark The Herald Angels Sing) and 2020 (Rise Up Shepherd and We Three Kings).

My arrangements were performed on a Korg SV-88 and recorded and edited with REAPER.

Piano Album: This Is My Story

Church Fundraising Project.

"This Is My Story" was originally released in audio CD format for the Guelph Citadel Salvation Army building project fund. Released on Nov. 7, 2002, this recording was made possible by the late Ted (Edward) Marshall of Marshall Arts Productions.

This project was the first of many opportunities to work closely with my friend. Ted recorded, edited, and mastered the project. He gave me my first real look into the world of sound engineering.

The editing process was eye-opening. While most of these tracks were edited (a couple heavily *cough*), one or two were complete takes. I just wish I remembered which ones!!

The album was recorded in the beautiful Humbercrest United Church sanctuary (Toronto, Canada) on their lovely grand piano in the summer of 2002. The album artwork was designed by Ted's son, Len Marshall, of Quiet City Design.

Voice

One of my favourite instruments is the easiest to carry: the voice.

While I have no formal voice training, I have benefited greatly from being surrounded by a lot of talented people who have mentored, taught, and encouraged me over the years. I also listen keenly whenever I'm accompanying and learn a lot from different adjudicators and conductors.

I didn't open my mouth to sing a solo until my sister's wedding reception. It was a huge surprise and nobody saw it coming. It was great fun - a novelty number which was top secret until reception. But, this first solo also started something because I learned how much I love to sing as a soloist.

While my vocal trio Marked By Love is featured throughout this portfolio, I also had the opportunity to sing alto in a wonderful Salvation Army SATB choral group called The Canadian Staff Songsters for its first seven years of (modern) existence.

Vocal Trio: Marked By Love albums

Selections from the Marked By Love albums. Things Thought Impossible (2007). A Light in the Dark (2011).

In the mid-2000s, I started writing and then performing some female vocal trio arrangements with a couple of friends from church. This snowballed into having a regular backing instrumental trio. Then naming the group Marked By Love... recording an album... singing all over Southern Ontario... and then recording a second album. The group had a good run of musical ministry for well over a decade.

The albums primarily feature my own compositions and arrangments. Both Marked By Love albums were recorded, editing, and mastered by Ted (Ed) Marshall of Marshall Arts Productions, using various multi-track techniques, allowing me to exercise my production and performance muscles in many different ways.

Both albums were recorded at The Salvation Army Guelph Citadel. Learn more about the group's history and music at on the Marked By Love website.

Instrumental

I've been playing the cornet (and subsequently the trumpet, flugelhorn, etc.) since I was around 7 years old. So, nearly as long as piano! With practical learning through Salvation Army programs at church, music schools/camps, and bands, I have been immersed in ensemble playing for most of my life.

I also have picked up other instruments through various opportunities over the years. While my favourite is likely percussion, my next most competent instrument after brass is the acoustic guitar.

Presented by Guelph Citadel Band on Dec. 6, 2014.

I had the opportunity to present a Christmas-themed cornet solo at an annual community carol sing held at St. George's Anglican Church, Guelph, with the support of the Guelph Citadel Band of The Salvation Army led by Bandmaster Allan Hicks. Guitar Accompaniment: All My Work

From the Marked By Love album A Light in the Dark.

In this re-imagining of the hymn "All My Work is for the Master", I created and performed a gentle fingerpicking guitar accompaniment, with light percussive hits on the guitar body. (The additional improvisational guitar work was added by Doug Elvin.)

A fun fact - the history of the guitar-accompanied Marked By Love trios is rooted in how I used to find it easier to accompany us live on a guitar than on the piano. This was also especially helpful when singing without our band at our church's Christmas Eve services.

Guitar Accompaniment: What Child is This?

From the Marked By Love album Things Thought Impossible.

This is the first ever guitar-accompanied Marked By Love arrangement. I created and performed a jazzy fingerpicked setting of one of my favourite carols, What Child Is This?

It's still one of my favourites to play when noodling around with a guitar.

Composition & Arrangements

Almost all of the Marked By Love vocal trios are original songs or arrangements written by me. While these songs are likely my most publicly known body of work, I have written a number of SATB choral arrangements, brass band compositions, worship songs, and piano arrangements. The only song that might surpass them all is a silly little campfire song called "The Potato Song". (Learn more about The Potato Song in the Media section.)

Here is a collection of some of my favourite compositions and arrangements from the Marked By Love albums.

Shepherds Watch

From the Marked By Love album A Light in the Dark.

This high-energy rendition of the traditional Christmas carol, While Shepherds Watched Their Flocks, features an original melody, an homage to the original melody, and brief cameo appearances by Handel's Hallelujah Chorus. Punched up by a brass section, this is a fun song to listen to and to perform.

Jesus Loves Me

From the Marked By Love album A Light in the Dark.

This is a funky setting of the familiar words of Jesus Loves Me. It features a brass section, a punchy new tune, and some a textural surprises near the end.

Things Thought Impossible

The title track from the Marked By Love album Things Thought Impossible.

An all original song and the first more produced arrangements of the group, including a jazz organ layer throughout, a trumpet feature at the end, and the iconic hand claps.

The Resurrection Day Song

From the Marked By Love album A Light in the Dark.

An all original song that inspired the title of the album, A Light in the Dark. Originally written for an Easter time Impact Brass anniversary concert, where Marked By Love was the special musical guest. One of the most complex Marked By Love arrangements, this featuring the standard band plus a required acoustic guitar part (played by me) and decorative electric guitar. (When performed live, it has also included a full SATB choir part.)

Potato Song Project - Virtual Campfire

A COVID-19 pandemic project from Summer 2020.

This video is the end goal of The Potato Song Project. This silly little campfire song I wrote many years ago seemed like a good outlet for working on some creative and technical skills while bringing people together. I am grateful to my friends who were willing to participate!

The big challenge for this type of project is the wide variety of video and audio production quality submitted. The aim was to create something that captured the authentic feel of a fun community campfire singalong and, despite the challenges, I think this video does just that.

A COVID-19 pandemic project from Summer 2020.

This lyric video of The Potato Song includes high contrast lyrics.

The reason I include it here is to focus of the audio production aspect of the song. All of the instumental and vocal parts were performed by me. I also recorded, mixed, and mastered the track.

Learn more about the project.

Potato Song Project - Choral Potatoes

A COVID-19 pandemic project from Summer 2020.

Choral Potatoes was one of the most fun, most indulgent videos of entire The Potato Song Project. Producing something a bit more quirky (...even more quirky?) than the standard videos was the focus.

Here, I wrote, performed, recorded, and mixed a choral treatment of The Potato Song. I also created the animation of the "Choral Potatoes" using GIMP and Camtasia 2019.

Our Prayers of Lament

A COVID-19 pandemic project from Spring 2021.

A more serious-natured project, Our Prayers of Lament focuses on expressing our hearts after one year into the COVID-19 pandemic in Ontario, Canada.

For this project, I did all of the video and audio editing. The piano track playing in the background is an improvised work of mine that was modified for this project. Learn more about the project.

Like The Potato Song virtual campfire before it, a big challenge for this project was the wide variety of video and audio production quality submitted by the participants. The aim was to create something that captured the authentic heart of a community of faith, and to do so in an artful and intimate way. I believe the end product reflects this and more.

Print

For many years, I have had opportunities to create print artifacts and have learned (sometimes the hard way) what works and what doesn't! Since the days of print software like PrintMaster and PrintShop, I have enjoyed designing things on a computer. I embrace opportunities to learn and experiment with design software as technology develops over time.

As my awareness of "the end user" or intended audience has developed more keenly with experience, my sense of design and purpose has also been influenced for the better. I find myself asking questions and testing print products much like in software development.

Sample from CD booklet

Marked By Love CD booklets.

I did the majority of the design work for both of the Marked By Love albums, with assistance on some specific details.

A less-than-fun fact - The cover of A Light in the Dark was a hard lesson learned, as I moved in haste to meet a deadline. Business Cards

Inner-City Home business cards.

I have been a remote technology volunteer for Inner-City Home of Sudbury, Ontario since January 2022.

My first print project for Inner-City was a business card redesign. I created cards for specific personnel and also a blank contact card with room for writing in a name or note.

Important features of the new design included the updated logo and motto (in both English and French) and both food bank locations. For this project, I used GIMP.

Donation Receipt
(Before & After)

Inner-City Home donation receipt.

Another print project for Inner-City Home of Sudbury was the donation receipt redesign. There were multiple receipt styles in use with various issues, such as not having enough room to write, not having good flow for the person filling it out, and not having the donation amounts right-aligned. The most recent version did not include the Inner-City logo or the bilingual name.

The redesign addressed these issues and added a little style and colour. For this project, I used Adobe Illustrator CC 2022.

Teaching

"Teachers open the door, but you must enter by yourself."

I have worked professionally as a college instructor, but I have been teaching all my life. Part of my natural tendency is to help others and to share information, so even as a child I was helping and teaching peers and adults alike. I started teaching music as a young teen, both privately and through church programs. Beginning in my twenties, I started teaching through Salvation Army music camps and also took on educational positions during my undergrad studies.

I bring a teacher's heart to every role and many skills have been grown and expanded simply for the joy of sharing with others.

The examples provided here are specifically tied to the educational side of my emerging brand, Foundational Things.
