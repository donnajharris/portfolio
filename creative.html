Piano

I can play a lot of instruments, but the piano is my primary and most proficient instrument without a doubt.

Playing since I could first reach its keyboard, I am classically-trained, with a Grade 10 Piano Certificate and Grade 3 and 4 Keyboard Harmony courses from the Royal Conversatory of Music (Toronto, Canada). I can play "by ear", from chord charts, lead sheets, and written manuscripts. Comfortable with a wide range of styles.

My main piano responsibilities continue as a long-time piano accompanist for The Salvation Army Guelph Citadel church services and church choir. I also have experience accompanying soloists for auditions, performaance juries, and Kiwanis Festivals.

Piano Solo: The Wounds of Christ

Virtual church piano selection, Easter 2020. This video was the first of many virtual church contributions from 2020-2022, arranged, rehearsed, recorded, and edited in the same little room.

My improvisational arrangement was performed on a Korg SV-88 and recorded and edited with REAPER. The video was created with Camtasia 2019.

Piano Album: Winter Storm Project

The "Winter Storm Project" of 2018 was a self-produced Christmas present for a group of my friends. I asked for their favourite carols and played improvisations with little to no editing. The styles were quite varied and the playlist reflects of the diversity.

Some of these tracks were used by The Salvation Army in Canada and Bermuda on their annual Christmas compilation recording in 2019 (What Child is This? and Hark The Herald Angels Sing) and 2020 (Rise Up Shepherd and We Three Kings).

My arrangements were performed on a Korg SV-88 and recorded and edited with REAPER.

Piano Album: This Is My Story

"This is My Story" was originally a physical audio CD release for the Guelph Citadel Salvation Army building project fund. Released on Nov. 7, 2002, this recording was made possible by the late Ted (Ed) Marshall of Marshall Arts Productions, my first of many opportunities to work with my friend. Ted recorded, edited, and mastered the project, and gave me my first deep insights into the world of sound engineering.

The editing process was eye-opening. While most tracks were edited (a couple, heavily *cough*) one or two were complete takes. I wish I remembered which ones!!

The album was recorded in Toronto on the lovely grand piano at Humbercrest United Church in the summer of 2002. The album artwork was designed by Ted's son, Len Marshall, of Quiet City Design.

Voice

One of my favourite instruments is the easiest to carry: the voice.

While I have no formal voice training, I have benefited greatly from being surrounded by a lot of talented people who have mentored, taught, and encouraged me over the years. I also listen keenly whenever I'm accompanying and learn a lot from different adjudicators and conductors.

I didn't open my mouth to sing a solo until my sister's wedding reception. It was a huge surprise and nobody saw it coming. It was great fun - a novelty number I wrote in secret. But it also started something... showing me how much I love to sing as a soloist.

I also had the opportunity to sing in a wonderful Salvation Army choral group called The Canadian Staff Songsters for its first seven years.

Vocal Trio: Marked By Love albums

Selections from the Marked By Love albums. Things Thought Impossible (2007). A Light in the Dark (2011).

In the mid-2000s, I started writing and then performing some female vocal trio arrangements with a couple of friends from church. This snowballed into having a regular backing instrumental trio. Then naming the group Marked By Love... recording an album... singing all over Southern Ontario... and then recording a second album. The group had a good run of musical ministry for well over a decade.

The albums primarily feature my own compositions and arrangments. Both Marked By Love albums were recorded, editing, and mastered by Ted (Ed) Marshall of Marshall Arts Productions, using various multi-track techniques, allowing me to exercise production and performance muscles in many directions.

Instrumental

I've been playing the cornet (and subsequently the trumpet, flugelhorn, etc.) since I was around 7 years old. So, nearly as long as piano! With practical learning through Salvation Army programs at church, music schools/camps, and bands, I have been immersed in ensemble playing for most of my life.

I also have picked up other instruments through various opportunities over the years. While my favourite is likely percussion, my next most competent instrument after brass is the acoustic guitar.

Cornet Solo: In the Bleak Midwinter

Presented by Guelph Citadel Band on Dec. 6, 2014.

I had the opportunity to present a Christmas-themed cornet solo at an annual community carol sing held at St. George's Anglican Church, Guelph, with the support of the Guelph Citadel Band of The Salvation Army led by Bandmaster Allan Hicks. The brass band arrangement of In the Bleak Midwinter (masterfully arranged by Trevor Worthington) was unknowingly (to me) recorded by friends in the front row in a rare moment where I forget to be nervous. All I remember was the beautiful sound of the church sanctuary and am greatful it was captured.

Guitar Accompaniment: All My Work

From the Marked By Love album A Light in the Dark.

In this re-imagining of the hymn "All My Work is for the Master", I created a gentle finger picking guitar accompaniment with light percussive hits on the guitar body. (The additional improvisational guitar work was added by Doug Elvin.)

A fun fact - the history of the guitar-accompanied Marked By Love trios is rooted in how it was easier (at the time) for me to accompany us live on guitar than it was on the piano, which was especially helpful when singing at Christmas Eve services.

Guitar Accompaniment: What Child is This?

From the Marked By Love album Things Thought Impossible.

The first guitar-accompanied Marked By Love arrangement, I created a jazzy finger picking setting of one of my favourite carols What Child Is This? It's still one of my favourites to play when noodling around with a guitar.

Composition & Arrangements

Almost all of the Marked By Love vocal trios are original songs or arrangements. While this is likely my most publicly known body of work, I have written a number of SATB choral arrangements, brass band compositions, worship songs, and piano arrangements. The only song that might surpass them all is a silly little campfire song called the Potato Song. (See more about The Potato song in the Media section.)

Here is a collection of some of my favourite compositions and arrangements from the Marked By Love albums.

Shepherds Watch

From the Marked By Love album A Light in the Dark.

A high-energetic rendition of the traditional Christmas carol While Shepherds Watched Their Flocks with an original melody, an homage to the original melody, and brief cameo appearances by Handel's Hallelujah Chorus. Punched up by a brass section, this is a fun song to listen to and to perform.

Jesus Loves Me

From the Marked By Love album A Light in the Dark.

This is a funky setting of the familiar words of Jesus Loves Me. It features a brass section and a punchy new tune some a textural surprises near the end.

Things Thought Impossible

The title track from the Marked By Love album Things Thought Impossible.

An all original song and the first more produced arrangements of the group, including a jazz organ layer throughout, a trumpet feature at the end, and the iconic hand claps.

The Resurrection Day Song

From the Marked By Love album A Light in the Dark.

An all original song that inspired the title of the album, A Light in the Dark. Originally written for an Easter time Impact Brass anniversary concert, where Marked By Love was the special musical guest. One of the most complex Marked By Love arrangements, this featuring the standard band plus a required acoustic guitar part (played by me) and decorative electric guitar. (When performed live, it has also included a full SATB choir part.)

Business Cards

Inner-City Home business cards.

I have been a remote technology volunteer for Inner City Home in Sudbury, Ontario since 2022. My first print project for Inner-City was the business card redesign. I created cards for specific personnel and also a blank contact card with room for writing in a name or note.

Key features of the new design included the updated logo and motto, in both English and French, and both food bank locations. For this project, I used GIMP.

Donation Receipt
(Before & After)

Inner-City Home donation receipt.

Another print project for Inner-City was the donation receipt redesign. There were multiple receipt styles in use, with various issues such as not having enough room to write, not having good flow for the person filling it out, and not having the donation amounts aligned. The most recent version did not include the Inner-City logo or the bilingual name.

The redesign addressed these issues and added a little style and colour. For this project, I used Adobe Illustrator.

Education / Teaching

I have worked professionally as a college instructor, but I have been teaching all my life. Part of my natural tendency is to help others and to share information, so even as a child I was helping and teaching peers and adults alike. I started teaching music as a young teen, both privately and through church programs. Beginning in my twenties, I started teaching through Salvation Army music camps and also took on educational positions during my undergrad studies.

I bring my a teacher's heart to every role and many skills have been grown and expanded for the joy of sharing with others.

The examples provided here are specifically tied to the educational side of my emerging brand, Foundational Things.
